Philadelphia Schools

The Philadelphia School District wanted to know which neighborhoods contain their largest schools that are easily accessible to the city-wide bike network in order to encourage its students to bike to school. This map identifies which neighborhoods contain schools within the school district, enroll more than 1,200 students, and exist within 0.1 mile of the bike network. I used the “Select by Attributes” tool in GIS to highlight schools in the Philadelphia School District that have more than 1200 students. Next, I used the “Select by Location” tool to highlight schools within 0.1 mile of a bike network from the schools with over 1200 students. Then, I used the “Select by Location” tool again to locate which neighborhoods contained the selected schools. Lastly, I used the layout tool to create a map of the selected schools, neighborhoods, and other elements.

Healthy Food Stores

The city of Philadelphia would like to know where to encourage development for healthy food stores. The areas’ identified are already targeted for investment (empowerment zone), accessible by public transportation, and not near farmers' markets or corner stores participating in the Healthy Corner Store initiative. This would allow for the largest impact for communities without access to healthy food. The applicable skills I used to create the map include: spatial joins, buffering and union, calculating geometry attributes, and layout/cartography. The ideal candidate area is the light blue polygon, near Temple University, at 9,568,004 sq feet. It's the largest contiguous area, making the possibility of development greater.

Pensylvania 2020 Election Results

This map displays the Presidential Election results for Pennsylvania in 2020. The map is color-coded in graduated colors to show the degree to which each county in Pennsylvania voted for Trump or Biden. The skills I used to create the map include: joins, select by attribute, calculate fields, symbology, and layout.
